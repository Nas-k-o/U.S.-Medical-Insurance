Medical Insurance in US Regions

Northwest, Northeast, Southwest, and Southeast

Northwest

The Northwest region, including Washington, Oregon, and Idaho, has a high focus on preventive care and wellness. Many residents benefit from employer-provided insurance, and the region sees lower-than-average uninsured rates due to robust Medicaid expansion efforts.

Northeast

The Northeast, with states like New York, Massachusetts, and Pennsylvania, has some of the most comprehensive healthcare systems. The region is known for high insurance premiums but offers extensive coverage options. Massachusetts was a pioneer in healthcare reform, leading to low uninsured rates.

Southwest

In the Southwest, states like Arizona, New Mexico, and parts of Texas face challenges with higher uninsured rates, especially in rural areas. Medicaid expansion has helped many, but the region still struggles with affordability and access in certain communities.

Southeast

The Southeast, including Florida, Georgia, and the Carolina's, has some of the highest uninsured rates in the country. Economic disparities and limited Medicaid expansion have contributed to this issue. However, insurance marketplaces have provided vital coverage to many residents.
